Question 1

Consider a consumer having a utility function \(U = 2x+y\) where x and y are two commodities. Suppose that the price of x and y are both Rs. 5 each and the consumer’s income is Rs. 100. In equilibrium, the consumer will consume the bundle

    A. (10,10)

    B. (0,20)

    C. (20,0)

    D. None of the above

Answer

Method 1

For \(U=2X+Y\) , \(\frac{\frac{\partial u}{\partial x}}{P_x} > \frac{\frac{\partial u}{\partial y}}{P_y}\)

So consumer will only consume Good X as Marginal benifit per unit of money spend on good X is more.

Method 2

Linear Utility Function \(\to\) Lagrange method does not work

\(P_xX+P_yY=100\) OR \(5X+5Y=100\)

Extreme Bundles

{X,Y}={0,20} and {20,0}

As \(U=2X+Y\)

U(0,20)= 2(0)+1(20)=20 Utils

U(20,0)=2(20)+1(0)=40 Utils

Hence \(U(0,20) > U(20,0)\)

Optimal Consumption is (X ,Y)=(20,0)
