Using data from a sample of sizen, the intercept and slope coefficients from an ordinary least squares regression of y on x, are a and b respectively. Which of the following is false?

  • \(\sum_{i=1}^{n} (y_i−a−bx_i)x_i= 0\)

  • \(\frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^{n} y_i=a+\frac{b}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n x_i\)

  • a and b are the solution to \(min_{α,β} \sum_{i=1}^{n} (y_i -\alpha-\beta x_i)^2 =0\)

  • a and b are the solution to \(min_{α,β} \sum_{i=1}^{n} |y_i -\alpha-\beta x_i| =0\)

  • \(\sum_{i=1}^{n} (y_i−a−bx_i)x_i\)

\(=\sum_{i=1}^{n} (y_i−a−bx_i)x_i\)

\(=\sum_{i=1}^{n} (y_i x_i-ax_i-bx_i^2)\)

\(=n\bar y \bar x-na\bar x-b\sum_{i=1}^n x_i^2)\)

\(\neq 0\)

  • \(\frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^{n} y_i=a+\frac{b}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n x_i\)

as we know \(y_i=a+bx_i\)

\(\sum y_i=\sum(a+bx_i)\)

\(\frac{1}{n} \sum y_i=\frac{1}{n} \sum (a+bx_i)= \frac{1}{n} (na+b\sum x_i)= a+\frac{b}{n}\sum x_i\)

  • a and b are coefficients , they are not likely to be solution.
